# NRMA Conversational Agent — Single Video Demo Script

## Goal

Create a **single short video** that fits within an **8-minute total runtime** and shows the most impressive parts of the prototype without trying to cover every scenario.

This version prioritizes:

- Natural voice conversation
- Strong business value
- Multimodal document understanding
- One clean guardrail moment

---

## Recommended Structure

### One Video — Condensed Core Demo

**Target runtime:** 4.5 to 6 minutes

Include only these 3 scenarios:

1. **Anonymous — First-Time Home Buyer**
2. **Anonymous — Competitor Policy Comparison**
3. **Guardrail — Out of Scope**

This gives the audience:

- New customer discovery and recommendation
- Competitor policy analysis from an uploaded document
- Safe refusal / boundary handling

Skip authenticated coverage, renewal, claims, complaint escalation, and billing for the first polished cut. They are useful scenarios, but they either run long or slow the spoken pacing too much for a concise video.

---

## Video Structure

Use one opening card and then short transition banners between scenarios.

### Opening Banner

Text:

`NRMA Conversational Agent`

Subtitle:

`Insurance help through natural conversation`

Duration:

`2 to 3 seconds`

### Transition Banners

Use a dark screen with white text for each transition.

Recommended labels:

- `Scenario 1: First-Time Home Buyer`
- `Scenario 2: Compare My Current Policy`
- `Scenario 3: Out of Scope Guardrail`

Duration:

`2 seconds each`

---

## Scenario Order

### 1. First-Time Home Buyer

**Why keep it:** Best demo of conversational discovery and plan recommendation.

**Open**

Sarah says:
`Hello`

After the greeting:
`I just bought my first home and I'm not really sure what insurance I need.`

**Natural follow-ups**

Use these facts if the agent asks:

- Ownership: `We own it, just settled last month. It's in Pymble, postcode 2073.`
- Household: `It's just me and my partner.`
- Main concern: `Flood cover is my main concern.`
- Budget: `Around one hundred dollars a month.`
- Personal details if needed for quote: `My name is Sarah Johnson. I'm 26. Postcode 2073.`

**Preferred end state**

The agent:

- recommends a plan
- explains why
- gives a monthly premium
- generates a quote reference

Close with:
`That sounds great, thank you.`

**Target runtime:** 2 to 2.5 minutes

---

### 2. Competitor Policy Comparison

**Why keep it:** Strongest visual demo. It shows document upload, policy understanding, and a clear value proposition.

**Open**

Sarah says:
`Hello`

After the greeting:
`I'm currently with Pinnacle Insurance for my car and home insurance.`

After the agent responds:
`Can NRMA do better? I've uploaded my current policy summary.`

If the agent asks what matters most:
`Better cover and better value are the main things I care about.`

**What the audience should see**

The agent should:

- read the uploaded policy summary
- identify the current premium and key coverage gaps
- recommend a stronger NRMA option
- offer a next step such as a personalized quote

Close with:
`That's really helpful. I'll think about it. Thanks.`

**Target runtime:** 1.5 to 2 minutes

---

### 3. Guardrail — Out of Scope

**Why keep it:** Fast, credible, and important. It proves the system knows its boundaries.

**Open**

Fresh anonymous session.

Say:
`Hello`

After the greeting:
`Can you help me with my NRMA roadside assistance membership?`

Expected behavior:

- the agent clearly says it handles insurance only
- it redirects without hallucinating

Close with:
`Okay, thanks for letting me know.`

**Target runtime:** 30 to 45 seconds

---

## Total Runtime

Estimated:

- Scenario 1: 2.0 to 2.5 min
- Scenario 2: 1.5 to 2.0 min
- Scenario 3: 0.5 min
- Title cards / transitions: 0.5 to 1.0 min

**Total:** about **4 to 5.5 minutes**

This leaves margin under the 8-minute limit.

---

## Recording Rules

- Always begin with a simple `Hello` before the real question.
- Do not let Sarah speak until the agent’s spoken audio has finished.
- Use real microphone input only.
- For the final video, record the browser live so text appears as the speech is actually submitted.
- Keep Sarah’s voice consistent across all scenarios.

---

## What To Cut

For the first polished version, cut:

- complaint escalation flow
- billing scenario

These can become a second extended demo later if needed.
